# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the **Awesome AI Tools Database** community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- ✅ Using welcoming and inclusive language
- ✅ Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- ✅ Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- ✅ Focusing on what is best for the community
- ✅ Showing empathy toward other community members
- ✅ Giving credit to contributors and their work

**Examples of unacceptable behavior:**

- ❌ Spam submissions or self-promotion without disclosure
- ❌ Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- ❌ Public or private harassment
- ❌ Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- ❌ Submitting fake affiliate links or misleading tool information
- ❌ Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Contribution Standards

In addition to general conduct, contributors are expected to:

1. **Be honest** about tool capabilities and free tier details
2. **Disclose** any personal affiliation with tools you submit
3. **Not submit** tools for personal gain without community benefit
4. **Respect** the review process — maintainers may decline submissions
5. **Follow** the formatting guidelines in [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject:
- Comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Tools submitted for spam or deceptive purposes

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening an issue or contacting the maintainers.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines:

### 1. Correction
**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional.
**Consequence:** A private, written warning with clarity around the nature of the violation.

### 2. Warning
**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.
**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards.
**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation, harassment, or spam.
**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.
